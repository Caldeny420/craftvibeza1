POPIA Compliance Statement

Effective date: 29 January 2026

LexPilot (Pty) Ltd is fully committed to compliance with the Protection of Personal Information Act 4 of 2013 (POPIA) across all services we provide to South African law firms.

1. Our Role under POPIA

We act as an operator (processor) for the majority of personal information processed on behalf of our clients, particularly in relation to:

  • Web development: firm branding assets, team bios, practice area descriptions, contact form submissions
  • Digital visibility & authority content: Google Business Profile data, review metadata, LinkedIn profile inputs, content topics and approvals
  • SaaS / practice management: client & matter identifiers, trust account metadata, invoicing records (processed strictly under client instructions and for compliance purposes)

2. Lawful Basis & Purpose Limitation

We process personal information only when necessary for:

  • Performance of our contract with you (delivering websites, content, SaaS)
  • Compliance with legal obligations (LPC rules, financial record-keeping)
  • Legitimate interests (service improvement, support), subject to balancing tests

We never process more data than required and always align with the original purpose or obtain fresh consent where needed.

3. Security Measures

We implement appropriate technical and organisational safeguards, including:

  • Encryption in transit (TLS 1.3) and at rest
  • Role-based access controls and audit logging
  • Regular vulnerability scanning and penetration testing
  • Exclusive data hosting in South Africa
  • Incident response plan with notification to clients and the Information Regulator as required by section 22

4. Data Processing Agreements (DPAs)

Every client engagement includes a formal, written Data Processing Agreement that covers:

  • Processing instructions and permitted purposes
  • Security obligations and sub-processor controls
  • Breach notification timelines
  • Data return or deletion upon termination
  • Audit and inspection rights

5. Sub-processors

We use vetted sub-processors bound by the same obligations we have to you. Current categories include:

  • Hosting & infrastructure (South African data centres)
  • Payment processing (e.g. PayFast)
  • Email & communication delivery

A current list is available on request.
