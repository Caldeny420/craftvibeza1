Promotion of Access to Information Act 2 of 2000

Private Body Manual – Section 51 • Last updated: 29 January 2026

1. Introduction & Particulars of LexPilot (Pty) Ltd

This manual is published in terms of section 51 of the Promotion of Access to Information Act 2 of 2000 (PAIA) and provides information on how to request access to records held by LexPilot (Pty) Ltd, a private body providing digital infrastructure services to South African law firms (websites, authority content, and SaaS for practice management).

3. Categories of Records Available Without Request (Automatic / Public Access)

The following records are freely available on our website without needing a formal PAIA request:

  • Marketing materials, service descriptions, and pricing information
  • Public policies: Privacy Policy, POPIA Compliance Statement, Disclaimers, Terms of Service
  • Website content (including this PAIA Manual)

4. Categories of Records Available on Request (Section 51(1)(c))

Other records may be requested under PAIA, subject to grounds of refusal (Chapter 4). Examples include:

  • Client contracts, engagement letters, and service agreements
  • Web development records (project briefs, content approvals, site specifications)
  • Digital visibility records (Google Business optimisation reports, content calendars, review facilitation logs)
  • SaaS-related records (processing logs, audit trails, anonymised usage data)
  • Internal policies, operational documents, and financial records (subject to confidentiality)

Access may be refused on grounds such as commercial confidentiality, protection of third-party privacy, legal privilege, or harm to commercial interests (PAIA Chapter 4).

5. Request Procedure

  1. Complete PAIA Form C (available from the Information Regulator website: inforegulator.org.za)
  2. Submit the completed form, together with proof of identity and any prescribed fee (if applicable), to: paia@lexpilot.co.za
  3. We will respond within 30 working days (extension possible under section 54 for complex requests)
  4. If refused, reasons will be provided in writing, together with your right to appeal or lodge a complaint with the Information Regulator

6. Fees

Fees may apply for search, reproduction, and preparation time as prescribed by PAIA regulations. A deposit may be required for large or complex requests. No fee is charged for personal requests (your own information).

7. Availability of this Manual

This manual is publicly available on our website at /paia-manual.html and can be inspected free of charge during business hours at our premises (by appointment).
