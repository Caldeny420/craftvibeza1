POPIA Compliance Statement

Last updated: January 14, 2026

Our Commitment to POPIA

LexPilot Accelerate is fully committed to complying with the Protection of Personal Information Act 4 of 2013 (POPIA) and its regulations. We process personal information responsibly, lawfully, and transparently. This statement outlines our approach to POPIA compliance in relation to our website, services, and client interactions.

1. Responsible Party

LexPilot Accelerate (owned and operated by Callum Denysschen) is the responsible party (controller) under POPIA for the personal information we collect and process. Contact details:

  • WhatsApp / Phone: +27 76 431 2871
  • Business address: Sandton, Johannesburg, Gauteng, South Africa

2. Information Officer

Callum Denysschen is the appointed Information Officer. For any POPIA-related queries, requests, or complaints, contact us directly via WhatsApp at +27 76 431 2871.

3. Lawful Basis for Processing

We process personal information only when we have a lawful basis under POPIA, including:

  • Performance of a contract (e.g. delivering Accelerate packages)
  • Legitimate interests (e.g. improving services, communicating with clients)
  • Consent (e.g. optional marketing communications – you can withdraw at any time)
  • Legal obligation (e.g. tax records, regulatory reporting)

4. Processing Principles

We adhere to the 8 conditions for lawful processing under POPIA:

  • Accountability – We take responsibility for compliance
  • Processing limitation – We collect only what is necessary
  • Purpose specification – We use information only for stated purposes
  • Further processing limitation – We do not repurpose without lawful basis
  • Information quality – We take steps to ensure accuracy
  • Openness – This statement and our Privacy Policy provide transparency
  • Security safeguards – Reasonable technical and organisational measures are in place
  • Data subject participation – You have rights to access, correct, object, etc.

5. Your Rights Under POPIA

As a data subject, you have the right to:

  • Be informed about processing of your information
  • Access your personal information
  • Request correction, deletion, or destruction
  • Object to processing (including for direct marketing)
  • Restrict processing in certain circumstances
  • Data portability (where technically feasible)
  • Withdraw consent (where consent is the basis)
  • Lodge a complaint with the Information Regulator

To exercise any right, contact us via WhatsApp at +27 76 431 2871. We will respond within 30 days (extendable under POPIA regulations).

6. Security Measures

We implement reasonable technical and organisational safeguards to protect personal information against loss, unauthorised access, disclosure, alteration, or destruction, including:

  • Secure communication channels (encrypted WhatsApp where possible)
  • Limited access to information (only founder has access)
  • Secure third-party processors with POPIA-compliant agreements
  • Regular review of security practices

7. Cross-Border Transfers

Some service providers (e.g. cloud hosting, analytics) may be outside South Africa. We ensure appropriate safeguards (e.g. binding contracts, adequacy decisions) for any cross-border flows in line with POPIA section 72.

8. Complaints & Dispute Resolution

If you are unhappy with how we handle your personal information:

  • First contact us directly via WhatsApp +27 76 431 2871 – we aim to resolve informally
  • If unresolved, you may lodge a complaint with the Information Regulator: inforeg@justice.gov.za | +27 10 023 5207

9. Updates to this Statement

We may update this POPIA Compliance Statement. Changes will be posted here with the updated date. Continued use of our services constitutes acceptance.

10. Contact

For POPIA queries, access requests, objections, or complaints: WhatsApp +27 76 431 2871 or reply to any message from us.

Information Regulator contact: inforeg@justice.gov.za | +27 10 023 5207
