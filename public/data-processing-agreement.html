POPIA-Compliant Data Processing Agreement

This Data Processing Agreement ("DPA") supplements the Terms of Service and Privacy Policy. It applies where LexPilot processes personal data on behalf of the Client (the "Firm") as a Processor under POPIA.

  • 1. Definitions
  • • "Personal Data": Any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person.
    • "Processing": Any operation performed on Personal Data.
    • "Processor": LexPilot (or its sub-processors).
    • "Controller": The Firm (as the data controller under POPIA).
  • 2. Processing Instructions
  • LexPilot processes Personal Data only on the Firm's documented instructions, including for providing the Service (billing, AI drafting, CPD tracking, trust alerts). The Firm instructs LexPilot to process data in South Africa only.
  • 3. Purpose & Scope
  • Processing is limited to: Firm management, client data input/output, billing, compliance alerts. Categories: Firm staff, clients (names, contacts, case details). No special categories (e.g., health, race) unless explicitly provided by the Firm.
  • 4. Security Measures
  • End-to-end encryption for all data in transit and at rest.
    Independent annual security audits.
    Strict access controls: Only authorised LexPilot team members under NDAs.
    Data stored solely on AWS Johannesburg region.
    Breach notification: Firm and Information Regulator within 72 hours.
  • 5. Sub-Processors
  • Authorised sub-processors:
    • PayFast (payments – Johannesburg servers)
    • Firebase (Google Cloud – Johannesburg region, auth/notifications only)
    • SendGrid/Mailgun (email delivery – POPIA compliant)
    • AWS (cloud storage – Johannesburg only)
    Full list available on request at privacy@lexpilot.co.za. Firm may object to any sub-processor with reasonable cause (30 days' notice). LexPilot will not engage objected sub-processors.
  • 6. Data Subject Rights
  • LexPilot assists the Firm in responding to data subject requests (access, rectification, deletion, objection) within 48 hours.
    The Firm remains responsible for authorising requests and handling direct communications.
    No direct data subject requests accepted by LexPilot without Firm instruction.
  • 7. Data Deletion & Return
  • Upon termination or request, LexPilot will:
    1. Return all Personal Data in structured CSV/JSON format within 7 days
    2. Delete all Personal Data (including backups) within 30 days
    3. Provide deletion certification upon request
    Subject to legal retention periods (tax, audit – max 5 years anonymised).
  • 8. Audits & Inspections
  • Firm (or appointed auditor) may audit LexPilot's compliance once per year.
    60 days' written notice required. Audits at Firm's expense (unless breach found).
    Remote audits preferred; on-site only with Regulator approval.
    LexPilot provides SOC 2 Type II reports and annual POPIA audit summaries.
  • 9. Governing Law
  • This DPA is governed by South African law (POPIA, Constitution). Disputes resolved exclusively in Johannesburg High Court or arbitration (AFSA rules).
  • 10. Duration & Termination
  • Effective for entire Service term + 5 years post-termination for confidentiality/deletion.
    Terminates automatically on main Terms of Service termination.
    Survives for data deletion, audits, liability.
